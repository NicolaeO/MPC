How to Open a Bank Account as a Newcomer

One of the very first things you need in the US is a bank account — you need it to receive your paycheck, pay rent, and build your financial life. The process is straightforward once you know what documents to bring and which banks work best for newcomers.

What You Need to Open an Account

Requirements vary by bank, but you will generally need:

  • Government-issued photo ID — your foreign passport is accepted everywhere. A US state ID or driver's license if you have one.
  • Social Security Number (SSN) or ITIN — most banks require one of these. If you do not have an SSN yet, see the ITIN section below.
  • US address — you need a mailing address. A friend's address, an apartment you just rented, or a PO Box may work. Banks need somewhere to send your debit card.
  • Initial deposit — anywhere from $0 (Chase, Chime, most online banks) to $25–$100 for some credit unions.

Don't Have an SSN Yet? Use an ITIN

An ITIN (Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is issued by the IRS to people who are not eligible for an SSN. Many immigrants use an ITIN while waiting for SSN eligibility or if they are on a visa that does not authorize work.

Banks that commonly accept ITIN in place of SSN:

  • Bank of America
  • Wells Fargo
  • TD Bank
  • US Bank
  • Citibank (varies by branch)
  • Chime (very newcomer-friendly, accepts ITIN)
Tip: Call ahead or check the bank's website before visiting. ITIN policies vary by branch and individual banker — if one branch says no, try a different branch or bank.

Step-by-Step: Opening Your First Account

1

Choose a bank. Chime or an online bank requires no in-person visit. Major banks require a branch visit for your first account.

2

Gather your documents: passport, SSN/ITIN, proof of address (utility bill, lease, or bank statement).

3

Visit the branch or apply online. Fill out the application, provide documents, and make the initial deposit.

4

Receive your debit card by mail in 5–10 business days. Activate it via the app or phone.

5

Set up direct deposit with your employer using your account and routing numbers from the bank's app.

What If You Are Denied?

Some people are denied because of a negative history in ChexSystems, a consumer reporting agency banks use to track past account issues (unpaid overdrafts, account fraud, etc.). If this applies to you:

  • Ask specifically for a second-chance checking account — many banks offer them
  • Try Chime — they do not use ChexSystems
  • Request your free ChexSystems report at consumerdebit.com to see what is reported

Types of Accounts to Open

  • Checking account — for daily spending, debit card, bill pay. Open this first.
  • Savings account — for your emergency fund and goals. At a minimum, open this at an online bank with a high-yield savings rate (4%+).
  • Credit card — once your account is open and you have some history, apply for a secured credit card to start building credit (see the credit score article).

Do Not Wait

Open a bank account within your first week in the US. Without one, you cannot receive a paycheck, cannot pay rent by check or ACH, and will rely on expensive money orders and check-cashing services. It takes 30 minutes to set up and changes everything.
