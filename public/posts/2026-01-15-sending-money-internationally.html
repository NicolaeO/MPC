Sending Money Back Home

Sending money to family in Moldova or elsewhere internationally is a regular need for many immigrants — and choosing the wrong service can cost you 3–7% of every transfer in hidden fees and bad exchange rates. Here is a comparison of the best options available in 2025.

The Hidden Cost: Exchange Rate Markup

Most services charge two ways: a transfer fee (visible) and an exchange rate markup (hidden). The markup is the difference between the real mid-market rate (what you see on Google or xe.com) and the rate the service offers you. A 3% markup on a $1,000 transfer means you lose $30 before any fees are applied.

Always compare the total recipient amount — not just the fee — when evaluating services.

Wise

Uses the real mid-market exchange rate with a transparent fee (typically 0.5–1%). Often the best value for bank-to-bank transfers. Fast (1–2 business days). Available for Moldova/Romania.

Wise.com

Remitly

Specializes in remittances to developing countries including Moldova and Romania. Offers Express (minutes) and Economy (3–5 days) speed tiers. Good promotional rates for first transfer.

Remitly.com

Western Union

Worldwide reach. Recipient can receive cash at an agent location — useful if they do not have a bank account. Higher fees than Wise, but convenient and trusted. Available in Moldova.

WesternUnion.com

PaySend

Good for transfers to Eastern Europe and Moldova. Flat fee transfers with competitive rates. Card-to-card transfers available — fast and simple for recipients with a debit card.

PaySend.com

What to Avoid

  • Bank wire transfers — typically charge $25–$45 per transfer plus a 2–4% exchange rate markup. The most expensive option for regular use.
  • Airport and hotel exchange kiosks — the worst rates you will find anywhere.
  • Cash sent by mail — not insured, not traceable, not safe.

Tax Considerations

Sending your own earned money to family is generally not taxable for you. However:

  • If you are sending money as a gift, gifts over $18,000 per recipient per year (2024) may require you to file IRS Form 709 (Gift Tax Return). No tax is owed until lifetime gifts exceed $13.6 million, but the form is still required.
  • Your recipient abroad generally does not pay US taxes on money you send them.
  • If you have foreign bank accounts that held over $10,000 at any point during the year, you must file an FBAR (FinCEN Form 114) separately from your tax return.

Staying Safe

Warning: Never send money to someone you have not met in person who is asking for urgent help, claiming to be in trouble, or offering an opportunity that requires payment upfront. Romance scams and "grandparent scams" often target immigrants who are less familiar with US fraud patterns. Once money leaves via wire transfer or gift card, it cannot be recovered.

Quick Recommendation

For regular transfers to Moldova or Romania, use Wise for bank-to-bank transfers or Remitly if your recipient needs cash pickup. Always compare the final received amount on both before sending — rates change daily.
