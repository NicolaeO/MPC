Careers You Can Start With Just a Course or Bootcamp

In the United States, many stable and well-paying professions do not require a four-year degree. A lot of immigrants — including those from Moldova — build strong careers by completing short courses, certifications, or specialized bootcamps that take weeks or months, not years.

Below are practical, high-demand career paths you can start quickly, even with paid programs.

1. Phlebotomy Technician

Training length: 4–12 weeks
Average pay: $18–24/hour

Red Cross Phlebotomy Course

2. Medical Assistant (MA)

Training length: 3–9 months
Average pay: $17–26/hour
AAMA — Becoming a Medical Assistant

3. Dental Assistant

Training length: 8–16 weeks
Average pay: $20–28/hour

Dental Assisting National Board

4. CDL Truck Driver

Training length: 4–8 weeks
Average pay: $1,000–$1,800/week

CFI Paid CDL Training

5. IT Support Technician / Helpdesk

Training length: 3–6 months
Average pay: $22–35/hour

Google IT Support Professional Certificate

6. Web Developer Bootcamp

Training length: 3–9 months
Average pay: $55k–$90k+

Web Developer Bootcamp by Colt Steele

7. HVAC Technician

Training length: 6 months – 1 year
Average pay: $24–40/hour

NITC HVAC Certification

8. Security Guard (PERC Card)

Training length: 1–2 weeks
Average pay: $15–22/hour

Illinois PERC Card Requirements

9. Real Estate Agent (Illinois)

Training length: ~60 hours
Average pay: Commission-based (many earn $70k+)

Illinois Real Estate Licensing Info

10. Esthetician / Cosmetology

Training length: 4–12 months
Average pay: $20–40/hour + tips

Beauty Schools Directory

Final Thoughts

Many immigrants build successful careers in the U.S. through short-term training. Whether you prefer medical, technical, or trade work, there are plenty of opportunities that can change your life without a 4-year degree.
