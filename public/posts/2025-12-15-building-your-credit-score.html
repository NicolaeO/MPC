Building Your Credit Score from Scratch

When you arrive in the US, you do not have bad credit — you have no credit history at all. This is a blank slate, and with the right moves you can build a good credit score in 6–12 months, which opens doors to apartments, auto loans, mortgages, and better credit card offers.

How the Credit Score Works

The most widely used score is the FICO Score, which ranges from 300 to 850. Lenders use it to decide whether to approve you for credit and at what interest rate.

300–579

Poor — very limited access to credit

580–669

Fair — some approvals, higher rates

670–739

Good — most credit products available

740–799

Very Good — competitive rates

800–850

Exceptional — best rates available

What Makes Up Your Score (FICO)

  • Payment History — 35% — the most important factor. Pay every bill on time, every time.
  • Credit Utilization — 30% — how much of your available credit you are using. Keep it below 30%, ideally below 10%.
  • Length of Credit History — 15% — older accounts help. Open your first account as soon as possible.
  • Credit Mix — 10% — having both revolving credit (credit cards) and installment loans (auto, student) helps slightly.
  • New Credit — 10% — every hard inquiry when you apply for credit temporarily lowers your score by a few points.

How to Build Credit from Zero

Secured Credit Card

You deposit $200–$500 as collateral which becomes your credit limit. Use it for small purchases and pay the full balance every month. After 6–12 months of good history, you may graduate to an unsecured card and get your deposit back.

Good options: Discover it Secured, Capital One Secured, Chime Credit Builder

Credit-Builder Loan

Offered by credit unions and apps like Self. You make small monthly payments, and at the end you receive the money you paid in (minus fees). The payments are reported to the credit bureaus, building your history.

Good options: Self Credit Builder, local credit union

Become an Authorized User

Ask a spouse, family member, or trusted friend with good credit to add you as an authorized user on their existing card. Their history on that card is added to your credit report. You do not even need to use the card.

Report Rent Payments

Rent is typically not reported to credit bureaus, but services like Experian RentBureau and Rental Kharma can report your on-time payments. This can help if you are paying rent anyway.

What Hurts Your Score

  • Late or missed payments — a single 30-day late payment can drop your score by 50–100 points
  • High utilization — using 80% of your credit limit signals risk to lenders
  • Closing old accounts — this shortens your average account age and reduces available credit
  • Applying for multiple cards at once — each hard inquiry costs a few points; space applications 6+ months apart
  • Accounts sent to collections — unpaid medical bills or phone contracts that go to collections are very damaging

Monitor Your Credit for Free

  • Credit Karma — free score and monitoring (TransUnion and Equifax)
  • Experian.com — free Experian score and monitoring
  • AnnualCreditReport.com — free full credit report from all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) once per year

The One Rule That Matters Most

Pay your credit card balance in full every month, on time, without exception. This one habit alone will build a great credit score over time. Set up autopay for the full statement balance so you never accidentally miss a payment.
