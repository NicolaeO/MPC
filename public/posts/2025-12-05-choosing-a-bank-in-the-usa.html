Choosing the Right Bank in the USA

Picking the right bank is one of the first and most important financial decisions you will make in the US. The options range from giant national banks to small credit unions to fully online institutions — each with real tradeoffs in fees, rates, and convenience.

Big National Banks

Banks like Chase, Bank of America, and Wells Fargo are the most recognizable names. They offer massive ATM and branch networks, robust mobile apps, and sign-up bonuses. The trade-off is monthly fees ($12–$15) that are waivable only with minimum balances or direct deposit, and savings account interest rates that are often near zero.

Chase

Largest US bank. Huge ATM network, excellent app, great sign-up bonuses ($200–$300 for new checking accounts). $12/mo fee waived with $500 direct deposit or $1,500 balance.

Bank of America

Nationwide branches, solid Preferred Rewards program with bonuses once you hit $20k+ in assets. Good for those who want everything in one place (banking, investing, credit cards).

Wells Fargo

Strong national presence with many branches. Good option for mortgages. $10/mo fee waived with $500 direct deposit. Generally on par with Chase and BofA.

Credit Unions

Credit unions are member-owned nonprofits. They consistently offer better savings rates, lower loan rates, and fewer fees than banks. The downside is that they are often local or regional, with smaller ATM networks — though many participate in the free CO-OP Network of 30,000+ ATMs.

To join, you typically need to meet a membership requirement — living in a certain area, working for a specific employer, or joining an affiliated organization. Many are open to the general public for a small one-time fee ($5–$25).

Best for: Auto loans, personal loans, and savings if you want the best rates. Search creditunions.org to find one near you.

Online Banks (Best Rates, No Fees)

Ally Bank

No monthly fees, no minimum balance. High-Yield Savings Account with competitive APY. No physical branches but excellent customer service and mobile app.

SoFi

Banking, investing, and student/personal loans in one app. High savings APY, no fees, up to 2-day early direct deposit. Good for young professionals who want everything in one place.

Marcus by Goldman

Excellent high-yield savings rates, CDs, and personal loans. No checking account. Good as a savings-only account alongside a checking account at a big bank or credit union.

Best for Newcomers and Those Building Credit

Chime

No credit check to open. No monthly fees, no overdraft fees. Gets your direct deposit up to 2 days early. Great first bank for immigrants and anyone starting fresh. Also offers a secured credit card to help build credit.

What to Look for in Any Bank

  • FDIC insured — protects your deposits up to $250,000 per account type. Non-negotiable.
  • No or low monthly fees — fees waivable with direct deposit or minimum balance
  • ATM network — or ATM fee reimbursements
  • High-yield savings rate — don't leave money in a 0.01% savings account
  • Mobile app quality — you will use this every day
  • Zelle integration — the most common way to send money between US bank accounts instantly

A Common Combination

Many professionals use Chase or a credit union for their primary checking (easy ATM access, branches when needed), paired with Ally or SoFi for savings where their money earns real interest. There is no rule that says you can only have one bank.
